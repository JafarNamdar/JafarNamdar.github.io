I am Postdoctoral Associate at the MIT Digital Supply Chain Transformation Lab. I recieved my Ph.D. in Business Analytics at Tippie College of Business, University of Iowa. I have advised by Professor Jennifer Blackhurst and Professor Gautam Pant. My research involves the application of econometrics, machine learning/AI, and operation research to derive insights about risks and disruptions in global supply chain networks.

My Ph.D. dissertation analyzes the vulnerability of global supply chain networks against disruptions and policy uncertainties. I show that industry may constitute many suppliers (low industrial concentrations) but concentrated in a single country (high geopolitical concentrations). Such geopolitical concentration makes strategies like diversifications and multiple sourcing ineffective if a natural disaster or a conflict hits the nation. Furthermore, I document that firms whose suppliers are operating in highly concentrated industries (geopolitically or industrially) experience 320 million U.S. dollars reduced in their sales.

In another chapter, I investigate how firms adjust their supply chains structures when policy uncertainties (PU) increase. I show that firms diversify their suppliers industrially, geopolitically, and geographically. Thus, when firms anticipate increases in PU, they diversify suppliers instead of strengthening existing relationships. I also explore how policy uncertainty affects firms' offshoring practices. The upstream PU intensifies firms' offshoring practices. Finally, I explore cross-sectional heterogeneity in the effect of PU on supply chain structures and supply chains performances.

I have been working on resilient supply network (RSCN) design problems since my Master's program. Before starting my Ph.D. at the University of Iowa, I was in the Industrial Engineering (IE) program. This means my earlier works are focused on applying Operation Research (OR) tools for designing supply networks that are resilient against disruptions yet optimized and efficient during normal situations.
